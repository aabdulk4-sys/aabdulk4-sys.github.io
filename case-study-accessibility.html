UX Research · Accessibility

CityConnect
Accessibility Audit

A comprehensive WCAG 2.1 accessibility audit and redesign of a municipal government services portal — making city services usable for all residents, including those with visual, motor, and cognitive disabilities.

Role
UX Researcher & Designer
Timeline
7 Weeks
Platform
Web (Desktop & Mobile)
Tools
Figma, axe DevTools, NVDA, Lighthouse
The Problem

Government services that 26% of residents couldn't actually use.

CityConnect, a fictional municipal portal for permit applications, utility payments, and event registrations, had never undergone an accessibility review. According to the CDC, 26% of U.S. adults live with some form of disability. For a government service — legally required to meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards under Section 508 — this wasn't just a design problem. It was a civil rights issue.

A preliminary Lighthouse audit scored the site 31/100 for accessibility. Screen reader testing with NVDA revealed that 6 of the 8 core service flows were completely unusable without a mouse. My goal was to audit the entire site, document every violation, prioritize fixes by severity, and redesign the 3 highest-impact flows to meet WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.

"I tried to pay my water bill online for months. I'm blind and the form wouldn't work with my screen reader, so I had to take a bus to City Hall every single time." — Participant in assistive technology testing session

Audit Process

Systematic evaluation across POUR principles

I structured the audit around WCAG 2.1's four POUR principles — Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust — using axe DevTools for automated scanning and manual testing with NVDA (screen reader), keyboard-only navigation, and 400% zoom for low-vision simulation.

I prioritized violations into three tiers — Critical (blocks task completion), Major (significantly impairs use), and Minor (inconvenience without blocking) — and documented each with the specific WCAG criterion, user impact, and recommended fix.

Critical
Form fields missing programmatic labels
6 form fields (permit application, utility payment) had no associated <label> elements — screen readers announced them as "unlabeled edit field"
WCAG 1.3.1
Critical
Keyboard focus trap in modal dialogs
Payment confirmation modals trapped keyboard focus, making it impossible for keyboard-only users to dismiss or complete the transaction
WCAG 2.1.2
Major
Color contrast ratio below 4.5:1
14 instances of text failing minimum contrast — including the primary navigation links at 2.8:1 against the gray header background
WCAG 1.4.3
Major
Error messages not associated with fields
Form validation errors displayed visually but not programmatically linked to input fields — screen reader users received no error feedback
WCAG 3.3.1
Minor
Missing skip navigation link
No "skip to main content" link — keyboard users must tab through all 18 navigation items on every page load
WCAG 2.4.1
Redesign

Accessible by default, not as an afterthought

I redesigned the three highest-impact flows — utility bill payment, permit application, and event registration — applying all critical and major fixes. Every form field received a visible, programmatically associated label. Focus management was implemented for all modal dialogs. Color contrast was brought to a minimum 4.5:1 ratio across all text. Error messages were linked to their fields using aria-describedby.

Beyond compliance, I simplified the information architecture of all three flows — reducing average steps from 6.3 to 3.8 — because accessibility and usability are not separate problems. Cognitive load reduction helps everyone, not just users with cognitive disabilities.

Outcomes

94/100 — and more importantly, real people could use it.

Post-redesign Lighthouse scoring reached 94/100 across the three priority flows. But the more meaningful metric: in follow-up testing with 4 assistive technology users — 2 using screen readers, 1 keyboard-only, 1 with low vision — all 4 successfully completed the utility payment flow independently, compared to 0 of 4 in the baseline test.
