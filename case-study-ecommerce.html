UX/UI Case Study · E-Commerce

ShopLocal
Marketplace Redesign

Redesigning a local artisan marketplace platform to reduce cart abandonment and help small business owners compete with Amazon by improving the discovery and checkout experience.

Role
UX/UI Designer
Timeline
6 Weeks
Platform
Web & Mobile
Tools
Figma, Optimal Workshop, Hotjar
The Problem

A 74% cart abandonment rate was killing small businesses.

ShopLocal, a Charlotte-based marketplace for local artisans and small businesses, had a cart abandonment rate of 74% — 14 points above the e-commerce industry average. Analytics showed users were dropping off at two specific points: the product discovery phase and the checkout flow. Sellers were losing thousands of dollars monthly to a broken UX.

The existing product pages lacked trust signals — no seller ratings, no clear return policies, and product photos were inconsistently sized and low resolution. The checkout flow required account creation before purchase, adding 3 unnecessary steps that drove impulse buyers away. My goal was to rebuild trust and remove friction at both critical drop-off points.

"I wanted to buy the candle but then it asked me to create an account and I thought — forget it, I'll just go to Etsy." — User interview participant, age 31

Research

Understanding what builds trust in online shopping

I ran a card sort with 15 participants using Optimal Workshop to understand how users mentally organize product information. I also conducted 8 contextual interviews, asking participants to shop for a specific item on ShopLocal while thinking aloud. Every hesitation, every moment of confusion, every abandoned tab was documented.

The card sort revealed users expected seller information — ratings, location, response time — to appear before product details, not after. This was the opposite of how the existing page was structured. Fixing the information hierarchy alone would address 40% of the identified trust issues.

Design & Iteration

Building trust through information hierarchy

I restructured the product page to lead with seller credibility: seller name, star rating, response time, and location above the product title. Product photos moved to a standardized grid with zoom capability. Return policy and shipping estimates were surfaced directly on the product page — not hidden in a footer link.

For checkout, I introduced a guest checkout path as the default option, with account creation offered only after purchase confirmation. This single change reduced the checkout flow from 7 steps to 4.

Results

Cart abandonment dropped by 31 points in 4 weeks.

After launching the redesigned product pages and checkout flow to 100% of users, ShopLocal tracked a 31-point reduction in cart abandonment — from 74% to 43% — within the first 4 weeks. Guest checkout accounted for 58% of completed purchases in the first month, validating that the account gate had been a major conversion killer.
